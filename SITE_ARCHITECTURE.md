# Hovoy GRE Pipe Site Architecture

## Positioning

Hovoy should present itself as an engineering-aware industrial supplier, not a catalogue site.
The site needs to guide buyers through three common starting points:

1. Product family is already known
2. Application is known but product path is not final
3. RFQ or supplier review is already in progress

## Homepage wireframe

1. Hero
   Clear positioning, two primary actions, proof points, and one industrial visual.
2. Entry routes
   Start by product family, application, documents, or RFQ.
3. Product families
   Five primary product cards with key use cases.
4. Application sectors
   Four sector cards tied to real service environments.
5. Commercial proof
   Experience, manufacturing, export coordination, and package-completeness signals.
6. Project workflow
   Inquiry review, technical alignment, quotation support, delivery coordination.
7. Contact CTA
   Sticky, visible, and simple.

## Product landing architecture

Product pages should follow the reading pattern established by major industrial manufacturers:
technical anchors first, product and joint visuals early, then applications, supply scope, proof, and RFQ.

### Product page wireframe

1. Breadcrumb and hero
   Product title, service positioning, quick actions, and one real product image.
2. Technical snapshot
   Pressure class, temperature, diameter range, jointing route, and service-media anchor points.
3. Product visuals
   Real product, installed pipeline, and connection-detail images.
4. Product overview
   Core use cases, evaluation points, and technical routes such as resin system, conductivity, and installation environment.
5. Application fit
   Typical operating environments and service media.
6. Jointing and fittings
   Connection systems, flange or joint details, and package items.
7. Supply scope
   Straight pipe, fittings, fabricated items, export packing, and project coordination.
8. Available documentation
   Datasheet path, route discussion, fittings clarification, and other download-ready material.
9. Manufacturing and test proof
   Testing capability, tooling, shipment readiness, and other real factory evidence.
10. RFQ and next actions
   Checklist, related applications, and a direct path to quotation.

## SEO principles for all pages

- Write only people-first, buyer-useful copy
- Tie keywords to applications, media, and project tasks
- Use internal links between products, applications, resources, and contact
- Add breadcrumb and product structured data where relevant
- Prefer pre-rendered or server-rendered HTML for indexable pages
- Add real proof assets: certifications, projects, factory, packing, inspection
